About Me

Kia Ora! My name is Bella.

I have been eating a plant based diet for five years now, discovering at fifteen the potential that cooking with non-animals products can have. I chose this lifestyle for many reasons, yet the greatest reward has been the culinary creativity I have gained through experimentation in the kitchen.

This change in diet meant that I was expected to cook, clean, and live independantly from a young age. Going into the adult world of juggling flatting, work, and university, these practical abilities have become a blessing. However, I realised that many of my peers have not gained the same culinary skills I have, and struggle with the shift from home cooked meals to dinner with the flatties. For these reasons, many students come to see cooking as a chore, which I wanted to change. Through this website, I hope fellow students will share in my passion for food, through fun and obtainable recipes aimed at those keen to keep learning.

As a student myself, I made this website with the goal of helping other hungry students through the week with fast and nutritious recipes. From my experience, breakfast and dinner are the trickiest meals in the busy life of a student. Breakfast is necessary to start off your day proactively, but as students we often look for a quick fix instead of a healthy meal. The plant based recipes I have provided are healthy, easy to make, and serve as brain food for those needing a pickmeup before their next big assignment. Dinner comes at the end of a long day, where your mind is too tired for thought and your body craves sustenance. My included dinner recipes are dependable, delicious, and the perfect reward to a productive day.

Hope you enjoy!

Classic Porridge

Ingredients

  • 1/2 Cup Oats
  • 1/2 cup favourite plant milk (I used oat)
  • 1/2 Cup of Water

Topping Ideas

  • Brown Sugar/Maple Syrup
  • Peanut Butter
  • 1 Banana
  • Chia Seeds
  • Shaved almonds
  1. Place all ingredients in a pot on medium heat, and stir for a few seconds until well combined.
  2. Let simmer for 5mins, stirring every minute or so until you have thick consistency.
  3. Once it has reached the desired constituency, take off heat.
  4. Place in a bowl and finish with your favourite toppings.

Tofu Wraps

Wrap Ingredients

  • 2 Wraps
  • Handful of greens (I use iceburg lettuce)
  • 1/2 Avocado
  • 1 Tomato
  • Vegan Mayo

Fried Tofu

  • 1/2 Block Firm Tofu
  • 3 Tbsp Soy Sauce
  • 2 Tbsp Seseme Oil
  • 1 Tbsp Siracha or chilli paste
  • 2 Tps Maple Syrup or Brown Sugar
  1. Prep your wrap ingredients. Slice the lettuce, cube the tomatoes and mash the avocado.
  2. Put aside and start on the tofu.
  3. For the tofu, combine the soy sauce, sriracha and maple syrup in a small bowl.
  4. Heat a pan over medium heat and add the sesame oil. While the oil is heating up, start cutting the tofu. I like to cut the tofu into 1cm x 1cm cubes.
  5. Once the oil is sufficiently heated, add the tofu to the pan and fry for around 5 mins or until golden brown.
  6. Add in your soy sauce mixture to the pan and let cook down for another few minutes.
  7. Take the tofu off the heat and start assemlbing your wrap ingredients.
  8. Place all ingredients in the wrap and finish off with a drizzle of vegan mayo.

Easy Mac n Cheeze

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of dry macaroni
  • 1/2 Cup Cornflour
  • 3/4 Cup Butter
  • 2 Cups Milk of choice (I use Oat)
  • 2/3 Cup Grated Vegan Cheese
  • 2 Tbsp Nutritional Yeast
  • 1 Tsp Smoked Paprika
  • Salt and Pepper to Taste
  1. Cook pasta as per package instruction
  2. Set a pan on medium heat and place butter in to melt
  3. Once butter is melted, add cornflour and whisk to combine
  4. Slowly add milk, whisking as you go
  5. Next add vegan cheese and nutritional yeast
  6. Once that is incorperated, add the remaining ingredients
  7. Let simmer for a few more minutes, before taking off heat
  8. Serve with a sprinkle of smoked paprika on top
